01 / Profile

About

I am a network and systems engineer specializing in the operational integration of quantum technologies into communication infrastructure. At the Deutsche Telekom Chair of Communication Networks, TU Dresden, my responsibilities span protocol design for a quantum-photonic testbed, sub-nanosecond time synchronization, and the orchestration software through which that testbed is operated as managed infrastructure rather than a laboratory apparatus.

That approach was formed in carrier operations. With Nokia's integration services I spent three years delivering IP/MPLS deployment and migration programmes: several hundred network elements commissioned, backbone capacity upgraded from 10/40 to 100/400 Gbit/s, and live traffic cutovers executed under near-zero service-impact constraints. I hold quantum systems to the standard that work demands — instrumentation, reproducibility and well-defined failure behaviour are requirements, not refinements.

03 / Quantum communication networks

Research

My doctoral research addresses a question network operators will eventually ask: how are entanglement, quantum-derived randomness and quantum-secured keys provisioned, measured and allocated by an operational network?

The published record spans entanglement utilization in quantum-classical networks, error-rate estimation in quantum key distribution, quantum entropy for cryptographic applications, quantum-resistant service architectures for the IoT, and the design tradeoffs of quantum time synchronization in industrial networks. Applied outputs include open5gs-qrandom-mod, an adaptation of the Open5GS 5G core that consumes quantum-derived randomness as an operational entropy source.
